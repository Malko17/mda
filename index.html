Hydrogeology Studies

Investigation of subsurface water resources, watershed delineation, drainage network analysis, and geological mapping to support sustainable groundwater exploration in the Adamawa region of Cameroon.

Hydrology

Watershed Delineation (Bassins Versants)

Delineation of drainage basins within the Djerem watershed using SRTM DEM data. The map highlights sub-basin boundaries, river network hierarchy (orders 1‚Äì6), and flow directions to characterize hydrological behaviour across the study area.

Geology

Geological Map of the Djerem Region

Geological mapping of the Djerem watershed identifying lithological units, rock formations, and structural features. This map forms the geological framework for assessing aquifer potential and groundwater recharge zones.

Drainage Analysis

Drainage Density Map

Spatial analysis of drainage density across the Djerem watershed (0‚Äì0.78 km/km¬≤), derived from SRTM data. Higher drainage densities indicate areas with lower infiltration capacity and greater surface runoff, informing recharge zone identification.

Structural Geology

Fracturing & Lineament Map

Extraction and analysis of structural lineaments from satellite imagery to identify fault zones and fracture networks. These features serve as preferential groundwater pathways and are critical targets for borehole siting in hard-rock aquifer systems.

Study Area

Localisation of the Danfili Study Zone

Geographic location map of the Danfili area, situating the study zone within its regional administrative and topographic context in northern Cameroon. This map anchors all hydrogeological and geophysical investigations conducted in the area.
